An athlete who knows exactly what you're missing

Four years of
incredible footage.
Zero time to use it.

Riley Loos spent four years at Stanford as one of the best gymnasts in the country. Four NCAA team championships. Seven All-American honors. A Senior Championship. A spot on the USAG national team.

Outside the gym, content kept sliding. Not because he didn't care — he did. But Management Science and Engineering, a year-round training schedule, and a competition calendar that had him traveling most weekends left no real window. The clips sat in his camera roll. The ideas never got further than ideas.

Then came surgery. And for the first time in years, Riley had space. He went back through years of footage — training sessions, competition moments, drills he'd been proud of — and built the system he never had time for during college. He started posting. The audience came fast.

Today, gymnastics is the job. Competing, creating, and the brand deals that follow.

"I had four years of incredible footage and zero time to do anything with it. Training, classes, travel, competing — by the time I had a free hour I didn't have the energy to sit down and edit. The clips just sat there.

It wasn't until after graduation — after surgery, honestly — that I finally had the space to go back through everything and start building. And it worked. The audience was there. The interest was there. I just needed the time and a system.

If Haymaker had existed when I was competing, I'd be in a completely different position right now. That's not a guess. That's just math."

Riley Loos · Stanford Alum · 4× NCAA Champion · USAG National Team · @rylitight
