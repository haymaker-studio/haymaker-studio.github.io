Our mission

To give every
college athlete

Not just the stars. Not just the revenue sports. Every athlete, every sport, every stage — with the same professional systems behind them.

The vision

NIL for every sport.

A world where NIL isn't reserved for the athletes already in the spotlight. Where the gymnast, the swimmer, the shot putter has the same access to professional content infrastructure as the quarterback.

Where building a personal brand doesn't require free time you don't have, skills you haven't learned, or connections you haven't made yet.

Haymaker is how that world gets built — one athlete at a time, at institutional scale.

Built by an athlete who figured it out

Ian Gunther didn't
build Haymaker
for athletes.
He built it as an athlete.

Ian Gunther is a Stanford alum, BS/MS, and 4× NCAA Champion. While competing full time, he built a following of 5 million and nearly 10 billion total views — not because he had extra time, but because he built a system efficient enough to work around a training schedule.

Our philosophy

Four things we believe
that shape everything
we build.

01

NIL should be
a system, not a
series of exceptions.

Most programs handle NIL case by case — one athlete, one deal, one moment at a time. There's no infrastructure behind it. No repeatability. No compounding value.

Haymaker is built differently. Every athlete who joins gets the same professional workflows, the same content systems, the same brand development process. It scales because it's designed to. The goal isn't to help a handful of athletes. It's to build something that works for every athlete in the program.

02

Every sport
deserves real
infrastructure.

Whether you have 500 followers or 50,000, Haymaker meets you where you are.

Athletes with existing audiences get professional systems to amplify and scale what's already working. Athletes starting from zero get real infrastructure to build their first meaningful presence. The tools don't change based on your sport or your starting point. Everyone gets the full system. NIL should be inclusive — not reserved for the athletes already in the room.

03

The goal isn't
the deal. It's
what you build.

One-off brand deals are fine. A personal brand that generates opportunities for the next ten years is better.

Haymaker isn't in the business of placing athletes in campaigns and moving on. We're in the business of helping athletes build audiences, develop their voice, and create something durable — content, reputation, reach — that belongs to them long after the last competition. The NIL revenue is a byproduct of building something real. We focus on the something real.

04

Your sport is
already worth
watching.

You don't need a personality transplant or a content strategy degree. The training footage, the competition moments, the access you already have as a college athlete — that's the raw material most creators would do anything to have.

Haymaker builds the system around it. You keep doing what you do. We make sure people see it.
