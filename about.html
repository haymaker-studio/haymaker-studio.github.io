Our mission

HAYMAKER

NIL shouldn't require you to become a content creator on top of being an athlete. Haymaker exists to handle the 15 hours a week that nobody in season actually has.

Co-founder, Haymaker

Hi, I'm Ian.

I'm a four-time NCAA champion and Stanford grad. I built a 5M following while I was competing — not after, during. Training sessions, finals week, all of it. Gymnastics was my whole life and I never wanted that to stop.

What I figured out is that social media was the way to keep it going. The following I built while competing became my career after it. Brand deals, partnerships, a platform — it all came from the audience I built while I was still an athlete.

Most athletes have everything it takes to do exactly that. The sport, the story, the access. What nobody has is 10 to 15 hours a week of editing, posting, and pitching that simply doesn't fit alongside a full training schedule and a full course load.

That's what Haymaker is. The team that handles those hours. The system I spent two years building by trial and error, now available to athletes from day one.

What we believe

Three things we believe
that shape everything
we build.

01

NIL should be
a system, not a
series of exceptions.

Every athlete who joins gets the same professional workflows, the same content systems, the same brand development process. Not just the stars. Not just the revenue sports.

02

The goal isn't
the deal. It's
what you build.

One-off brand deals are fine. A following that generates opportunities for the next ten years is better.

The NIL revenue is a byproduct of building something real. We focus on the something real.

03

It's not software.
It's a team.

We don't hand you an app and wish you luck. A crew shows up to your practices. Content posts every week. Brand deals arrive with terms attached. The system runs while you compete.

Haymaker is what I wish I had.
Now it's yours.
