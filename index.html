Here's how it works.

01
We show up.

Your practices. Every week, in-season and out. You don't think about filming.

02
We teach you what to capture.

The crew isn't there daily — your story is. We show you how to see your life as content — what moments matter, how to frame them, what your audience actually wants to watch beyond the sport.

03
We activate your archive.

Years of footage in your camera roll. Training clips, game moments, content you never used. We turn it into months of pipeline. You don't start from zero.

04
We produce everything.

Editing, captions, platform strategy. Every week. Championship week or injury recovery — the machine runs. You never open an editing app.

05
You review.

Open the Haymaker app. See what we produced. Tap approve or request changes. Your name, your voice, your call.

06
Brand deals come to you.

Brands pay athletes for sponsored posts. We bring the deal to your app — terms included. You say yes, no, or negotiate. We handle the rest.

You spend under an hour a week. Everything else is us.

Why it works.

Consistency is the product.

Content strategies fail because they depend on athletes having good weeks.

Finding time, remembering to film, feeling motivated, having ideas, being healthy, not traveling, not in finals, not overwhelmed.

Haymaker doesn't depend on any of that. We produce every week regardless. Consistency builds audiences. Consistency is what brands pay for. Consistency is the product.

Brand deals are real.
Most athletes run out of eligibility before they land one.

Not because they weren't good enough. Not because brands didn't want them. Because building the following that unlocks those deals takes hundreds of hours nobody in season actually has.

Ian had those hours because he was obsessed. Most athletes won't — and shouldn't have to. So he built the system that does it instead.
